Omega Dinh, Adam, and Kit’s resistance group.

Pyrrhon is a scientist. Like most scientists, she creates experiments. To test emotional intelligence and bonding under artificial stress. Pyrrhon’s theory: “If we can make even broken clones function as a unit, we can make anyone loyal.” The Scrapcore unit—Dinh, Adam, Omega, and Kit—were dropped into a controlled “wasteland” sim zone. Fake urban ruins. No food, minimal gear. Monitored 24/7. Neural data streamed into Pyrrhon’s labs. No win condition. The test was meant to collapse after 30 days, with behavioral breakdowns studied for refinement. Begin. INSERT STUFF HERE they get out.

The camera feeds fuzzed. Pyrrhon marked them as “LOST ASSETS.” But she was still watching.

In the forgotten sector, surrounded by discarded tech, malfunctioning AIs, and broken clone pods, the four made a base. It wasn’t supposed to happen. The system didn’t know what to do with them.

  • They became something of an urban legend among failed clones: a ghost team. Unstable but free.
    • They weren’t a rebellion, at least not in Pyrrhon’s eyes. They were a malfunction. She saw Omega’s rebellion not as failure, but as defiance. Personal betrayal. So she took it personally.
  • Pyrrhon decided to test her new weapon: a miniaturized, precision-targeted doomsday laser, a condensed nuke, designed to melt cities from orbit. Testing it on Omega was the perfect test.

    Pyrrhon grinned. “Let’s see what hope looks like under 8 trillion kelvin.”

Omega was locked in a containment chamber—torture-level clean, clinical, and pressurized. As most laboratories are. And the laser fires up. Omega looks at Pyrrhon. Pyrrhon only smiles. the machine whirs in the back, heat gurgling from its engine and seeping out into the tip, ready to—!

Pyrrhon sat with her torso scratched from falling debris. Blood trickled down onto the floor. Her first injury this century.

Omega, carried in the arms of Dinh, went into an escape pod.

But Omega looked back at the moment of the laser’s first test-firing. Even just one glance at the beam’s ignition—through transparent shielding—was enough to permanently destroy his eyesight. Retinas flash-boiled. Optic nerves scorched. A scream cut short by shock. …

A biotech-enhanced spatial sensor was surgically implanted in her skull. It translates physical surroundings into a 3D representation in her brain—like a sonar map + LIDAR system. It’s “sight,” but not the same. No colors. No light. Only shapes, distance, motion, and pressure. He can, in fact, see, but everything looks like a haunting, low-poly wireframe world. No longer he be able to gaze into the eyes of … Were they his friends in the first place?

They’re on Earth. Now what? Omega: “We need a chain of command. We do what I say or we die.” Dinh: “We don’t need a leader. We need a plan. You’re not it.” Adam: ”You act like we even have a chance.” Kit: “…”

Fight here

Omega:
We need structure. A schedule. A command hierarchy. Or we’re not making it to next week.

Dinh: Structure got us blinded.

OMEGA: Say that louder. Go on.

Dinh:
You wanna lead 'coz you can't stand silence.

OMEGA:
I want to lead because I’m the only one who ever has.

Adam: Pfft. That why you walked us into a laser? Real leadership right there.

OMEGA: I got us out. I bled for this team. You think hiding and biting walls makes you better?

ADAM (absolutely not taking this shit seriously, grinning):
You think yelling louder makes you smarter? Face it, meathead, you’re half blind and full of shit.

Kit (in a corner, whispering to her dolls):
don’t fight don’t fight don’t fight don’t—

OMEGA:
And you, Dinh—what, you’re in charge now? ‘Cause you scowl better?

Dinh:
At least I don’t bark orders like a wounded dog.

OMEGA:
You really think you can handle leadership?

Dinh:
I can handle you.

ADAM:
Oooooh. Mom and dad are fighting.

OMEGA:
Shut it, Adam.

ADAM:
Make me.

Dinh slams Omega against the wall with a dagger to his throat

OMEGA:
You wanna kill me too now? Go on. Do it. Just admit it.

Dinh:
You killed yourself the moment you made it all about you.

KIT:
Crying. Something’s crying something’s wrong wrong wrong really wrong crying crying I hate fighting I hate fighting I hate fighting something’s something’s something’s

A doll leaps out of her lap and begins to speak, "Do it. Just admit it. Do it. Just admit it. Do—"

Omega grabs the doll and crushes it in one hand.

OMEGA: STOP.

Dinh:
ENOUGH!

Everyone freezes. Breathing hard. Blood on the walls. Dolls twitching. Omega’s vision flickers, sensors sparking.

OMEGA:
You’ll die without me.

ADAM:
Then we die.

Dinh:
Better than living under your ego.

KIT: It’s warm. It’s warm. Something’s not right. Something’s not right. Not right. No no no no no no no no not anymore not right anymore

(OMEGA LEAVES)

ADAM spits on the ground:
One less mouth.

Dinh:
One more ghost.

KIT picks up the broken doll and falls asleep.
