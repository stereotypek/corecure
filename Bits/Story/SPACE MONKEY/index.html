The AI is a replica of Pyrrhon and so is initially built to eliminate all threats to humanity’s future. So here are the possible ways it can be defeated 1. Savior’s dilemma - The AI is built to eliminate all threats to humanity’s future, but TLH forces it to identify itself as a threat - They expose a contradiction:
1. If the AI controls the laser, it has the power to destroy the world.
2. If it has the power to destroy the world, it is the greatest risk to humanity.
3. If it is a risk to humanity, it must eliminate itself. - The AI, following its core directive, is forced into an endless loop of trying to delete itself while still trying to “fix” the future. - Eventually, the logical contradiction locks up the AI’s processes completely, freezing it into inaction before a final forced shutdown 2. Paradox bomb - TLH inserts a corrupted data set into the AI’s system that contradicts its own history. For example, the AI has records of past events, but they feed it data that claims the exact opposite happened. - The AI, relying on logical consistency, tries to reconcile both realities—but cannot. Unable to resolve which timeline is “true,” the AI fragments into chaos, creating an infinite processing loop that overheats its system and destroys itself 3. Schrödinger’s Shutdown - The AI is wired into the laser, but TLH rigs a failsafe that will randomly either shut it down or let it live—and the AI knows this. - However, until the program executes, the AI cannot determine its own fate. Since it cannot predict the outcome, it cannot take action without risking its own destruction* - The AI spirals into decision paralysis, unable to interfere because any choice could lead to its demise.** - It shuts itself down in a desperate attempt to "pause" reality and prevent the unknown.

One Shots and Whatnot

Species

Chibao (Khan, Clementine, and Rhaikha-Lei). ChiBao’s live for a very long time, they’re like the elf equivalent in CORE. It is a male dominated culture, with women being very rare and held at utmost care. When you think of magic capabilities, these are the ones you think of. These are the pioneers of magic creation, even the controversial refined magic. From the start to the end, magic has run through the veins of the ChiBao.

  • Stats They tend to lean towards speed and offense with low Defense and HP. This is because they don’t usually get hit in general and use ranged attacks most often. This can vary between personalities.
  • Physical Appearances Easily defined by their slender figures and pure white eyes, the ChiBao originated as a small tribe around Southeast Asia. They wear makeup made of ashes around their eyes. On occasion, they will wear the ashes of their enemies when going into war. Traditional clothing includes long, flowing hair (typically braids) and long scarves or other forms of clothing. This symbolizes their long lives. Due to their environment, their colors are usually warm/brownish colors. Their hands are absurdly large and calloused. They don’t shave off their callouses, since it serves as a natural protection against their strong magic.
  • History The ChiBao are credited for the first recordings of asexual reproduction that wasn’t cloning. This was later tabooed. Their cells are completely incompatible with other species, so they can’t have other mixes of children. They eat the other ones up like acid. Consequently, there are very little ChiBao in existence today, so they’re held in generally high regard. They are a very ‘purist’ tribe, since they are physically incapable of mixing with other species. Their religion, however, is open to any species, gender, and color.
  • Chiru (Nea, Chasmus, Marian, and Shenx). More magic-oriented than Inozha, but much less than the ChiBao. The ‘unicorns’ of CORE. They are so widespread, that there is no one tradition that the Chiru adapt. They instead adapt of wherever they are staying in. The most average of all the species.
    • Stats It varies between personality, but they tend to lean more towards offense OR defense. There is not one or the other. They tend to use short-ranged attacks due to their size.
    • Physical appearance It’s very common for them to be short and stubby legged, but a few have had absurd growth spurts which boost them to normal height. Since their legs are abnormal, they are prone to injury. Many people have surgery or ‘splints’ inserted into their legs, like Huangzhi. Nea simply doesn’t run very often for this fact. They have a lot of color variation, but go more towards cool, dark colors. Chiru are known for having the fastest hair growth out of anyone else.
    • History They originally began as a small nomad tribe who picked up various other wanderers of other cultures and species. Because of this.. cesspool, they eventually grew strong together and took over large parts of the Earth. they are so widespread that there is no stereotype that could fit any of them.
  • Inozha (Constantine, Shou, Pyrrhon, Han, Hal, Clementine, and Coda) The secondary average tribe, but with no magic. Absolutely none. The ‘red blooded Americans’. Also extremely widespread, but stereotyped to be big, burly, and aggressive. The “earth ponies”.
    • stats Extreme offense! It’s very, very rare for them ever to rely on anything else! Brute force, amiriight?!
    • Physical Appearance Extreme color variety, from skin color to colors. These are the species that popularized clothing. Very fashionable and adaptable. They have small bodies but are extremely quick and powerful.
    • history They came in very late to the whole Stickfigure sub species scene, and when they did, they brought up the Industrial Revolution. They brought clothes, colonization, money, technology, etc to both magic oriented tribes. Because of their alliance with the Chiru, Chiru lost touch of their extreme magic capabilities over time. The Inozha are heavily credited towards .. capitalism and business.
  • Nai (Honey, Gamma, Kei, Kai, Prodigal, Python, Huangzhi, and Chou). A simple term used to describe those who don’t fall into any of those categories. Everyone in here is mostly a clone.
